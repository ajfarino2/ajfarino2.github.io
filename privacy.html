Privacy Policy for TuneTransit

Last Updated: April 23, 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how Anthony Farino ("Developer", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and handles your information when you use the TuneTransit mobile application (the "App").

1. Information We Collect

We collect information necessary to provide the core functionality of TuneTransit, which involves converting music links and playlists between supported services (Apple Music and Spotify) and providing related features. The types of information include:

  • Music Service Information: When you connect your Spotify account, we securely store authentication tokens provided by Spotify in your device's Keychain to allow the App to interact with Spotify on your behalf (e.g., fetch playback state, create playlists). We do not store your Spotify password. We may fetch and store your Spotify User ID locally in the Keychain to interact with Spotify's playlist features. The App requires permission to access your Apple Music library and activity to perform conversions originating from Apple Music, fetch catalog details for matching songs/albums, and provide discovery features based on your listening history. This access is managed through standard iOS permissions. The App may access the currently playing song information from Apple Music or Spotify to display it on the home screen and offer quick transfer functionality.
  • User-Provided Information: URLs for songs, albums, or playlists that you manually enter, paste from the clipboard, or share with the App via the Share Extension.
  • Conversion & Usage Data: Details about the music being converted (song title, artist, album, artwork URL, ISRC/UPC codes where available) and the resulting links for the destination platform are processed during conversion. We store a history of your successful conversions (including metadata like song title, artist, album, artwork URL, platform links, and timestamp) locally on your device using Core Data. This history is used for features like the "Discover" tab. You can optionally save specific conversions. This saved data (similar to history data) is stored locally on your device using UserDefaults. A counter for your total number of transfers is stored locally on your device using AppStorage.
  • Daily Transfer Limits (CloudKit): For users on the free tier ("TuneTransit Day Pass"), we use Apple's CloudKit technology to track daily transfer usage to enforce limits. Specifically, we store a record in your private iCloud database containing your daily transfer count and the date the count was last reset. This record is associated with your iCloud User Record ID, but this ID is not stored or accessed by the Developer directly. This data resides within your personal iCloud space, managed by Apple, and is used solely for the purpose of resetting the daily transfer limit each day. The Developer cannot directly view or access individual user counts stored in private databases.
  • Device Information: A unique identifier for your device (identifierForVendor) may be sent to our secure backend server solely for the purpose of validating requests when fetching API keys necessary for app functionality. This identifier is not used for tracking purposes otherwise. We may send the app version along with the device identifier when fetching API keys.
  • API Keys: API keys required to communicate with third-party services (Spotify, YouTube) are fetched from our secure backend and stored securely in your device's Keychain.
  • Purchase Information: If you make In-App Purchases (e.g., for TuneTransit Premium), purchase status and history are processed and managed through Apple's StoreKit framework. We receive verification of purchase status (entitlement) but do not directly handle or store your payment information.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information solely for the following purposes:

  • To Provide App Functionality: Authenticating with music services, fetching music metadata, performing link/playlist conversions, searching for matches across platforms, displaying playback state, storing your history and saved items, providing discovery recommendations.
  • To Enforce Usage Limits: Utilizing CloudKit in your private database to track and reset daily transfer counts for free tier users.
  • To Authenticate Your Accounts: Connecting to your Spotify account securely.
  • To Improve User Experience: Storing preferences like your default startup screen.
  • To Secure API Access: Using device identifiers to validate requests for essential API keys from our backend.
  • To Manage Purchases: Verifying and restoring In-App Purchases via StoreKit.

3. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell your personal information. We only share information as necessary to provide the App's functionality:

  • Music Services (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube): Information like song titles, artist names, album names, ISRCs, or UPCs are sent to the respective services' APIs to find matches and perform conversions. Authentication tokens are used to interact with Spotify on your behalf. When using the "Open in YouTube" feature, a search query is sent to the YouTube API. Your use of these third-party services is subject to their respective privacy policies.
  • Apple Services: We utilize Apple technologies like Keychain (for secure storage), Core Data and UserDefaults (for local storage), CloudKit (for daily usage counts in your private database), and StoreKit (for In-App Purchases). Apple's Privacy Policy governs their handling of data.
  • Our Backend: Your device identifier and app version are sent to our secure server solely to authenticate requests for API keys needed by the app.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, subpoena, or other legal process.

4. Data Storage and Security

  • Local Storage: Conversion history, saved conversions, and preferences are stored locally on your device using Core Data and UserDefaults.
  • Keychain: Sensitive data like Spotify authentication tokens and third-party API keys are stored securely in your device's Keychain. An entitlement status flag derived from your purchase status may also be stored in the shared Keychain to allow the iMessage extension to verify premium access.
  • CloudKit: Daily usage counts for free tier users are stored in your private iCloud database, managed by Apple. This data is not directly accessible by the Developer.
  • Security: We use standard security measures like the Keychain to protect sensitive information stored by the App. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

5. Your Rights and Choices

  • Access and Deletion: You can view and delete your saved conversions within the App. You can clear your entire locally stored conversion history via the App's Settings screen. Disconnecting your Spotify account in the App's Settings will remove the associated tokens from the Keychain. Data stored in your private CloudKit database (daily usage counts) is managed via your iCloud account settings with Apple; the App does not provide a direct interface to delete these specific records.
  • Permissions: You can manage the App's access to your Apple Music library and activity at any time through your device's iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Media & Apple Music.
  • Clipboard: The app may detect music URLs on your clipboard to suggest them for conversion. This check happens locally on your device. You can choose to use or ignore the suggestion.

6. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy within the App. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.
